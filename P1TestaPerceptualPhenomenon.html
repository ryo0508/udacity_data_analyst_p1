1. What is our independent variable? What is our dependent variable?

Independent variable is the conditions: a congruent words condition and an incongruent words condition. Dependent variable is the time in seconds to finish reading the list.

2. What is an appropriate set of hypotheses for this task? What kind of statistical test do you expect to perform? Justify your choices. Now it’s your chance to try out the Stroop task for yourself. Now, download this dataset which contains results from a number of participants in the task. Each row of the dataset contains the performance for one participant, with the first number their results on the congruent task and the second number their performance on the incongruent task.

In this scenario, we will put null hypothesis as there is no difference in the average time in seconds to finish reading the list whether the lists are congruent words or incongruent words, which leads to our alternative hypothesis of there is difference in the average time in seconds to finish reading the list depending on the condition for the list. In order to statistically analyze the data set, we will conduct two-tailed t-test. It is also worth noting this is a dependent test since the subject design is conditional.

5. Now, perform the statistical test and report your results. What is your confidence level and your critical statistic value? Do you reject the null hypothesis or fail to reject it? Come to a conclusion in terms of the experiment task. Did the results match up with your expectations?

In order to test the statistical significance between the two data, we will conduct two-tailed dependent t-value test.

First we find the mean values of each sample.

Congruent: 14.05

Incongruent: 22.02

This gives us the point of estimate of 7.97. We would like to know whether this value sits within the confidence interval. Also at the same time we can find out the standard deviation of difference, which equals to 4.87.

We can find the t-critical value 2.064, where the sample data sets of degree of freedom of 23(=24-1) with confidence level of 95%

We can calculate t-value by:

t-value = 7.97 / (4.87/sqrt(24)) = 8.02

P-value is less than 0.0001.

Since 8.02 > 2.064 and p-value is less than 0.0001, we will reject null hypothesis at confidence level of 95%.

Detailed calculation was done in this spreadsheet.

This result aligns with what we could observe from the histogram we drew in #4, which we infer incongruent word lists requires more time to read.

6. Optional: What do you think is responsible for the effects observed? Can you think of an alternative or similar task that would result in a similar effect? Some research about the problem will be helpful for thinking about these two questions!

Human brain tends to make decisions not only from spelling of the words, but also from various sources like colors. Because what is written and the color it is written in does not match, brain gets confused and takes more time to process. We can assume that human brains makes decisions automatically without reading the actual words.

Based on the theory, we should be able to see the same type of observations by having a tester read bunch of fruit names. Each fruit names or vegetable names should be written in completely random colors. e.g. apple, banana, carrot, cabbage, peach
